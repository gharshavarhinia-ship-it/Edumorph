ENVIRONMENT POLLUTION - Types, Causes and Effects
Pollution is the introduction of harmful or unwanted substances into the
natural environment that cause damage to air, water, soil, and living
organisms.
These substances, known as pollutants, can be chemical,
physical, or biological in nature.
Pollution disrupts the balance of
ecosystems, affects climate patterns, and creates serious risks to human
health and wildlife.
With increasing industrialization and urbanization,
environmental pollution has become one of the most critical global
problems.
Types of Environmental Pollution
Air pollution occurs when harmful gases, smoke, dust, and particulate
matter are released into the atmosphere from vehicles, factories, power
plants, and burning of fossil fuels.
These pollutants reduce air quality
and lead to respiratory diseases such as asthma and bronchitis.
Water
pollution happens when rivers, lakes, oceans, and groundwater are
contaminated by sewage, industrial waste, oil spills, and plastic
materials.

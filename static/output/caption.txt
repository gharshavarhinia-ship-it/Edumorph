Environmental pollution is the introduction of harmful substances into our air, water, and soil, disrupting the delicate balance of our planet's ecosystems. Air pollution stems from vehicles and factories releasing toxic gases and smoke, leading to serious respiratory diseases like asthma and bronchitis in humans. Water pollution occurs when sewage, plastics, and industrial waste contaminate our oceans and rivers, harming aquatic life and making water unsafe for drinking. Soil pollution is caused by excessive pesticides and toxic waste dumping, which reduces land fertility and poses a major threat to global food production. With rapid industrialization, pollution has become a critical global problem. Understanding these causes is the first step toward protecting our health and wildlife.
